Ping Practice

Last updated: September 29, 2024

This Privacy Policy (“Privacy Policy” or “Policy”) explains how the Ping Practice Foundation (“We”) collects, uses, and shares information we receive from you through your use of the Ping Practice app (“App”) and https://pingpractice.org website (“Website”). This Privacy Policy does not apply to any third-party websites, services, or applications, even if they are accessible through our Services.

Introduction

It is essential to understand that, by using the Ping Practice app and https://pingpractice.org website, you consent to the collection, transfer, processing, storage, disclosure, and use of information you, and the device(s) you use, share as described in this Privacy Policy. That means that reading this policy carefully is important.

Types of Information We Receive From You & How We Gather It

Ping Practice invites you to be in conversation with yourself. As such, we do not collect, or have access to, any of the pings you capture.

Information we do gather…

Through communicating with us: You may provide information like your name, email address, area of study, motivation for practicing, and other information that may relate to you or help identify you.

Through using the Ping Practice app: Information is automatically sent by your device. This includes information like IP Address, user agent information, network and connectivity information, device identifiers, language preferences, software and operating system names and versions, and hardware model.

How We Use Information We Receive From You

We use your information for the purpose of pursuing our charitable mission, including:

  • Operating the Ping Practice App and Website
  • Making the App and Website more useful and ergonomic
  • Experimenting with new ideas for the purpose of making practicing with Ping Practice more impactful
  • Sending emails with updates and communications about items we believe may interest you
  • Inviting you to share feedback

How We Do Not Use Information We Receive From You

We do not sell or rent your personal information, nor do we give it to others to sell you anything.

What Happens When We Make Changes To This Policy

Like most natural things, this Policy will change over time to accurately reflect Ping Practice practices and the law.

When we make changes to this Policy, we will notify you of those changes by, at a minimum, updating the pingpractice.org website and revising the "Last Updated" date above.

In the event that we need to make substantial changes, we will provide the proposed changes to people using Ping Practice (“Practitioners”) for an open comment period lasting fourteen (14) calendar days.

For minor changes, such as grammatical fixes, administrative or legal changes, or corrections of inaccurate statements, we will post the changes, revise the “Last Updated” date above, and offer a way for Practitioners to see what has changed.

If you have questions or suggestions about this Privacy Policy, or the information collected under this Privacy Policy, please email us at privacy@pingpractice.org.
